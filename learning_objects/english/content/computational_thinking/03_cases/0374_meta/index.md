<context>
**Problem Statement**<br>
There is a fault in the electrical installation. Fix this fault.
</context>
<decomposition>
**Decomposition**<br>
<ul>
    <li>Divide the electrical installation into its different subsystems.</li>
    <li>Isolate the problem area.</li>
    <li>Identify the various circuits and wiring in the electrical installation.</li>
    <li>Analyze each circuit individually.</li>
    <li>Identify the specific area causing the problem.</li>
</ul>
</decomposition>
<patternRecognition>
**Pattern Recognition**<br>
Troubleshooting often involves identifying patterns in data or behavior. These patterns can range from recurring problems to trends indicating potential causes:<br>
<ul>
    <li>Recurring issues may indicate an underlying cause.
        <ul>
            <li>Problems occurring repeatedly in the same location may relate to the environment or wiring at that location.</li>
        </ul>
    </li>
    <li>Abnormal measurements or signals from instruments (multimeters, oscilloscopes) can indicate the source of the problem.</li>
    <li>Identify relationships between problem occurrence and system changes, such as new components or software updates.</li>
    <li>Recognizing specific sequences as potential causes helps isolate the problem. Faults may be linked to certain sequences of actions or events.</li>
    <li>Apply previous knowledge and solutions to similar situations.
        <ul>
            <li>Recognize problems for which standard procedures exist.</li>
            <li>Adapt successful strategies for similar issues.</li>
            <li>Use understanding of causes and effects in electrical systems to analyze comparable situations.</li>
            <li>Generalize experiences with frequently failing components to troubleshoot faster.</li>
        </ul>
    </li>
    <li>Fundamental electrical principles can be applied broadly to provide a base understanding.</li>
</ul>
</patternRecognition>
<abstraction>
**Abstraction**<br>
<ul>
    <li>Understanding complex electrical systems requires abstraction, focusing on high-level concepts and relationships rather than every detail.</li>
    <li>Reading an electrical diagram requires interpreting abstract representations of components, connections, and functions.</li>
    <li>Troubleshooting involves interpreting numbers and symbols (e.g., multimeter readings, visual indicators) to understand the system's state.
        <ul>
            <li>Safety symbols are abstract representations of concrete warnings or actions.</li>
        </ul>
    </li>
</ul>
</abstraction>
<algorithms>
**Algorithmic Thinking**<br>
Troubleshooting follows a structured approach to identify the problem's cause, resembling an algorithm: visual inspection, measurements, logical tests, and implementing solutions.
<ul>
    <li>Systematic elimination: analyze the whole system, then inspect specific components.</li>
    <li>Logical tests help exclude certain components or connections as possible causes.</li>
    <li>Follow a specific order when taking measurements to ensure reliable results.</li>
    <li>Use algorithmic thinking to prioritize potential solutions by probability and impact.</li>
    <li>Safety procedures are structured steps to minimize risk and ensure consistent, effective action.</li>
</ul>
</algorithms>
