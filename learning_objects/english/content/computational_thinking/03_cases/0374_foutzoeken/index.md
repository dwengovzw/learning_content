# Troubleshooting in an electrical installation

**The students must fix a fault in an electrical installation.**

Electrical installations, e.g., one that supplies electricity to different parts of a building, can have multiple functions and subsystems, such as lighting, outlets, switches, protective devices, etc. They typically consist of different circuits and wiring. Electrical systems are therefore often complex. When a problem occurs, it is not always easy to solve it. A solid approach is called for.<br>
The students learn why troubleshooting is important in electrical installations and become familiar with the general troubleshooting process. They also learn how to keep themselves and the equipment safe during troubleshooting. They also become familiar with standard procedures or best practices for specific problems.

**Audience:** Students who work with electrical installations.

**Subject:** ...

**Prerequisites:** The students know the basics of electricity required for the installation in question. They can recognize the functions and subsystems, the different circuits, and the wiring.
