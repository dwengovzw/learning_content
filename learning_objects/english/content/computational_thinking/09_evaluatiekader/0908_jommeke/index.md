# Assessment framework

## Bias in AI systems based on coloring Jommeke

**Educational objective:**<br>
Students analyze the impact of digital systems on society from the principles of computational thinking.

**Teacher preparation:** 
- Fill in the CD concepts schema to make the link with the basic concepts of computational thinking clear.

**Learning objective to be assessed:** Students analyze the output of the AI system, linked to its bias, and see the connection with the impact of this system on society.

**Assignments for the students:**
- Turing test: identify whether an image is artificially generated or not.
- Discuss findings and arguments during a whole-class instructional conversation.
- Create a drawing that can bypass the AI system.
- Short presentation on the impact of the AI system on society.

**Assessment scheme:**
![Coloring Jommeke](embed/spinjommeke.png)
  
**Assignments for the students and proposed form of assessment:**
- Turing test: identify whether an image is artificially generated or not & Participation: done/not done (quantitative & formative).
- Discuss findings and arguments during a whole-class instructional conversation & Whole-class (1-2-3) exit ticket (qualitative & formative).
- Create a drawing that can bypass the AI system & Assignment: successful/unsuccessful (quantitative & formative).
- Short presentation on the impact of the AI system on society & Assignment: document (qualitative & formative).
